What Is Ideation and How Can It Benefit Your Organization’s Product Innovation?

To stay competitive, companies must innovate new and improved products that can solve or alleviate customers’ pain points. And it only takes one innovative idea to get the ball rolling on a breakthrough solution that can drive market share improvement.

However, product innovation isn’t about selecting the first idea that “sounds good” to the company’s CEO or leadership team. The key to executing product innovation lies in discovering and evaluating meaningful concepts through ideation.

What Is Ideation?

Ideation is the process of generating fresh ideas that can be transformed into innovative business solutions. And in the context of product innovation, ideation sets the foundation for everything leading up to a product launch.

The ideation process is generally comprised of the following elements***:

• Stage 1: Idea Generation: Introduce ideas that are aimed at solving a problem or satisfying a need, are driven by customer insights (analytics, market research, etc.), and leverage your company’s core capabilities.
• Stage 2: Idea Selection: Filter down the ideas generated based on features and functions with the highest priority.
• Stage 3: Idea Evaluation: Determine the feasibility of transforming your best idea(s) into new or improved products that will engage your target customers.

Through ideation, you can uncover answers to the following product innovation questions:

• What are your target customers’ biggest problems?
• What product can alleviate your target customers’ pain points?
• What insights, research, and analytics data support product innovation?
• How will your target customers engage with the product?
• What impact will the product have on customer behavior?

Note: *Keep in mind that this classical approach may restrict innovation, which is why experienced digital agencies tailor the ideation process according to their clients’ needs.

Who Should Attend Your Ideation Sessions?

The answer to this question depends on the size of the company and the type of product being considered for development. However, to come up with the most creative mix of ideas, it’s advisable to involve a diverse set of participants.

Potential attendees could include members of your design, development, sales/marketing, customer service, and project management team. Other potential parties you can involve are your customers and third-party vendors you work with on a regular basis.

As to question of “how many people should attend?,” an ideation session should ideally involve 5 to 10 people. Facilitation sessions can also run from several hours to several days, depending on the complexity of the product.

Should Your Ideation Sessions be Facilitated by a Third Party?

Facilitation sessions should always be facilitated by someone who can get the best ideas out of everyone while handling disagreements in a respectful manner.

Finding an ideation facilitator who can skillfully handle these tasks in an impartial manner can be a challenge — especially if interdepartmental rivalries exist within the company. However, ideation sessions facilitated through an experienced third-party innovation consultant can be a viable option.

Digital consultants with experience in managing ideation sessions can conduct the market research needed to understand a company’s competitive environment, capabilities, and business challenges from a holistic and objective perspective.

While it’s important to evaluate a vendor’s ideation process before engaging them, it’s even more important to select an ideation facilitator who is collaborative and acknowledges everyone’s ideas.

After all, participants who trust their ideation host are more likely to share their ideas — and one of those ideas just might lead to your company’s next breakthrough product.
