What Is Search Engine Optimization (SEO) and How Does It Work?

A website is more than just a collection of webpages describing an organization’s brand, products, and services. With proper optimization, a company’s website can become a powerful online sales tool that drives web traffic, brand awareness, and sales leads.

Unfortunately, too many companies believe that attractive web design and brand recognition are enough to generate traffic — which is a huge mistake.

Because when it comes to optimizing a website so prospective customers can find it online — nothing works better than Search Engine Optimization (SEO).

What Is Search Engine Optimization (SEO)?

According to Rand Fishkin, SEO expert and founder of the marketing software and analytics company Moz:

“SEO is the practice of increasing the quantity and quality of the traffic that you earn through organic results in search engines.”

Traffic quantity refers to how many people are visiting your website through search engines such as Google (e.g. People Google product X and then click on your company’s website from the search engine results (SERPs) page).

The quality refers to how valuable your web visitors are (e.g. People who visit your website because they want to evaluate your products/services represent high-quality traffic while random visitors who have no intention of making a purchase represent low-quality traffic).

Organic results represent search engine traffic that is built through SEO best practices instead of paid search engine traffic (e.g. bidding on search engine ads via Google Adwords).

How Does SEO Work?

Search engines “crawl” the web, gathering and indexing content found throughout the Internet — including information that’s on your company’s website.

That indexed content is then analyzed with an algorithm that “ranks” it according to how relevant it is to a user’s search engine query.

While search engines don’t reveal how they weigh certain SEO elements, ranking depends heavily on two types of optimization:

• On-page optimization: Conducting keyword research, and optimizing your web content, metadata (title tags, meta description tags, and heading tags), and internal link anchor text.

• Off-page optimization: Build links by having other websites and blogs link back to your web pages, which can build up your site’s credibility and SEO ranking on SERPs pages.

Note: It’s much easier to optimize your website’s on-page elements, but off-page optimization has a greater impact on your SEO ranking. Creating and sharing insightful content (e.g. articles, case studies, infographics, etc.), guestblogging, and testimonials are good ways to improve your off-page optimization.

Why Does Your Website Need SEO?

There’s one major reason why your organization’s website needs SEO — to make it easier for prospective customers to find your website so they can learn about your products, services, and brand.

And the easiest way for prospective customers to find your website is to rank high for search keywords that appear on the first page of the SERP — that’s because 60% of all organic clicks go to the top 3 results in SERPs.

If you’re still unconvinced about why your website needs SEO, consider the following insights from Accenture’s 2014 State of B2B Procurement Study:

• 94% of B2B buyers conduct online research before purchasing a product
• 84% of B2B buyers visit a supplier’s website when conducting online product research
• 77% of B2B buyers use Google to conduct online product research
• 55% of B2B buyers conduct online research for at least half of their corporate purchases

A Final Note About SEO

There are steps you can take right now to improve your SEO performance such as optimizing your website’s on-page elements — but don’t expect results overnight!

SEO isn’t a one-time action, it’s a ongoing process that must be managed continuously either in-house or through a reputable SEO or digital innovation agency that can analyze, improve, and keep you up-to-date on your SEO situation.

With an SEO plan of action that includes on-page optimization and content creation to improve off-page optimization, it will take 3 to 6 months before you start see real improvement in the quantity and quality of your web traffic.
