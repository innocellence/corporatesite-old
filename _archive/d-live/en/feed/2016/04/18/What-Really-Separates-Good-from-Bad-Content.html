Content Marketing: What Really Separates Good from Bad Content?

Companies that consistently share valuable content through their websites and/or blogs are more likely to generate more brand engagement, website traffic, and sales leads than those that don’t.

That’s why 76% of business-to-business (B2B) marketers plan on producing more content for consumers in 2016. But with so many companies creating new content, some will inevitably make the mistake of focusing too much on quantity over quality.

And if there’s one important principle that organizations developing a content marketing strategy need to grasp — it’s that quality matters.

Here’s what everyone involved with content creation needs to know about what separates good from bad content:

What Distinguishes Good from Bad Content?

Effective content marketing provides customers with the information they need to make better decisions — from choosing the right product or service to making better health and lifestyle choices.

And while the purpose of content marketing is to drive profitable customer actions, companies should never forget that customers want content that serves their needs — not the needs of the company. So what really distinguishes good from bad content?

Final Note on Content Quality

The question of whether or not quantity is better than quality is something many companies struggle to answer.

A company that focuses on quality may benefit from better engagement and lead quality by publishing engaging, detailed, and informative content — but there may not be a dramatic or consistent increase in web traffic or lead quantity.

Ideally, companies should strike a balance between quality and quantity by creating a mix of short- and long-form articles that satisfy the “good” content criteria above.
