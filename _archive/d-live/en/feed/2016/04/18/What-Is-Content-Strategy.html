What Is Content Strategy

18 April 2016 |
InnoCellence Systems Pte. Ltd.

What Is a Content Strategy and How Can Your Organization Create One?

Today’s customers don’t want hard-sell interactions with sales representatives — they want useful content that can help them make better buying decisions.

And the best way to give them the information they want is through an effective content strategy. Unfortunately, too many companies still rely on traditional sales methods that restrict the flow of valuable content to consumers.

That’s a huge mistake when you consider that according to Demand Gen’s 2015 Content Preferences Survey, 67% of consumers rely on online content research to make informed buying decisions.

The bottom line is this — if you want to satisfy your customers’ desire for content in the digital age, you need to develop a content strategy.

What Is a Content Strategy?

Let’s start by making the distinction between a content strategy and a content marketing strategy. A content marketing strategy deals with tactical level execution — creating and distributing content that builds brand awareness, engages prospects, and converts them into customers.

A content strategy deals with strategic level planning — assessing your team’s valuable editorial and technical input to achieve a holistic understanding of your company’s content capabilities, needs, goals, and lifecycle management.

Creating a cohesive strategy that satisfies your company’s content needs is important for the following reasons:

1. To achieve company-wide alignment on content purposes and goals
2. To improve the quality and efficiency of content produced
3. To produce the right content for your target audience(s)
4. 4. To enhance your brand awareness, reputation, and loyalty
5. To generate more sales leads for your company’s products/services

How Can Your Organization Create a Content Strategy?

Creating a content strategy is a team effort that requires editorial and technical input, and a keen understanding of the content lifecycle. To develop a content strategy, you need the following elements:

The Editorial Perspective
The editorial perspective comes primarily from your company’s content marketer or anyone involved in the content creation process (e.g. writer, editor, marketing manager, social media marketing manager, etc.).

Editorial input enables you to get answers to these key questions:

• Are there multiple user personas you should create content for?
• What kinds of content do your customers prefer?
• What customer engagement goals do you have for your content?
• How will you engage and funnel prospects through the customer journey?

A knowledgeable and experienced content marketer, writer, or editor can get the answers needed to create targeted and engaging and content.

The Technological Perspective
The technological perspective comes primarily from your company’s user experience (UX), analytics, and digital channel specialists (e.g. UX/UI designer, SEO specialist, digital marketing manager, etc.)

Technological input enables you to get answers to these key questions:

• What tools will we use to measure and improve content effectiveness?
• Which content types and topics perform the best among our target audience(s)?
• Which content distribution channels are most effective at delivering different types of content (e.g. videos, articles, etc.)?
• What behaviors and technologies do customers use to complete certain tasks (e.g. downloading a white paper, filling out an enquiry form, etc.)

Remember — quality content is nothing without the channels to share it and the tools to measure and improve it. Knowledgeable UX design, digital marketing, and innovation consultation professionals can help you get the technological perspective.

The Content Lifecycle
The content lifecycle is the driving force behind every successful content strategy. You cannot implement a content strategy effectively without understanding the content lifecycle — the continuous process by which content is planned, created, managed, measured, and optimized.

Here are the stages of the content lifecycle:

1. Planning: Create a strategic plan that oulines what kinds of content will be created, who will create that content, if the content will be localized for other markets, SEO optimization of content, channels for publication, and what goals do you want your content to achieve (e.g. drive sales leads).

2. Workflow: Implement a content workflow that clearly defines the processes and responsibilities for creating, editing, approving content for publication.

3. Management: Manage the publication, storage, and revision of published content. Having a good content management system (CMS) can simplify many of these processes.

4. Optimization: Monitor the performance of published content to evaluate goal achievement and to optimize content by determining which channels, devices, and types of content drive customer engagement.

The most important thing to remember is that it is a continuous process — insights gained from the optimization phase of the content lifecycle should be incorporated into the planning phase of the content lifecycle.

What’s Your Content Strategy?

Developing a content strategy can be a daunting task, especially if your company lacks the in-house editorial, marketing, and technical expertise needed to do it right.

If your company is in the process of finding the right personnel to fit these roles, freelancers can be hired to help you get your content strategy off the ground.

However, a better option would be to partner with an experienced digital innovation agency that understands your company’s content needs AND create engaging content content for your target audience(s).
