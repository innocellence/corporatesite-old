What Is Digital Analytics and Why Is It an Absolute Business Necessity?

18 April 2016 |
InnoCellence Systems Pte. Ltd.

What Is Digital Analytics and Why Is It an Absolute Business Necessity?

Companies that can’t uncover the needs, preferences, and habits of their customers are at a huge disadvantage in any market. Because in today’s digital business environment, the company that understands its target customers better than the competition, is the company with the most power to improve its market share.

But with digital analytics, companies can get the customer insights they need to improve their business strategies and optimize key marketing assests sush as websites, mobile applications, and content.

What Is Digital Analytics?

Digital analytics is the measurement and evaluation of qualitative and quantitative data collected by a company in order to improve:

A) A) The user experience (UX) the company delivers to customers and prospective customers online (e.g. web traffic growth, user-friendly navigation, etc.)

B) B) The user engagement of the company’s online content (e.g. web, blog, etc.)

C) C) The conversion rate of the company’s key online business objectives (e.g. getting more users to fill out a contact forms, making purchases, etc.)

Through digital analytics, companies can gain the actionable insights needed to track, measure, and achieve their desired business objectives.

How Can Digital Analytics Improve Business Outcomes?

When it comes to digital analytics, it doesn’t matter how much data you collect — if you can’t relate captured data to specific business goals and objectives — it’s meaningless.

For example:

A pharmaceutical company wanted optimize its global marketing resource allocation to maximze market growth for a new product.

Traditionally, the pharma took the inefficient approach of blanketing target markets with marketing dollars and waiting on consumer response before allocating resources.

But with analytics, the pharma was able to discover and predict which markets would show the greatest demand for its new product — leading to increased market growth and improved marketing return on investment (ROI).

While goals and objectives vary from company to company, some of the most common include:

  • **Market Research:** understanding and predicting the needs, preferences, and expectations of your target customers.

  • **Brand Improvement:** increasing brand awareness, engagement, and customer retention

  • **Sales Growth:** driving sales of your company’s products and services, especially if your business runs an eCommerce website

  • **Lead Generation:** collecting valuable prospect data that your sales team can use to convert leads into customers

  • **Content Engagement:** improving the number of views, shares, return users, and time spent viewing your content, especially if your company runs a blog

  • **Customer Support:** providing customers and prospective customers with informative content that supports them at every stage of the buying cycle. To track your progress towards achieving your business goals, it’s important to create a measurement plan that outlines what actions and metrics will help you reach your objectives.
